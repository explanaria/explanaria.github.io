Questions I Didn't Answer


  • How do we know that we can always make coordinate charts everywhere on a manifold?
    (To avoid this problem, we just define "a manifold is a space where you can always make coordinate systems".)
  • What happens if we look at paths moving around the surface of a manifold?
  • A Klein bottle can't exist in R3 without self-intersecting, but it can in R4. If you have an n-manifold, what's the smallest Rm it can exist in without self-intersecting?
  • How do we know that two sets of coordinate systems represent the same manifold?
    • (How do you define "same manifold"? Studying that is the realm of topology.)
  • What if instead of real numbers, we made each coordinate system out of complex numbers?
    (This is called a "symplectic" manifold! They pop up in physics a lot!)
  • Our universe sure seems like a 3-manifold. Which 3-manifold do we live in?
    (Mathematicians who study this question call themselves "physicists".)
